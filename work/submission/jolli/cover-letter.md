Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "Internalizing Common-Noun Identity
Criteria with Quotient Higher Inductive Types," for publication in the *Journal
of Logic, Language and Information*.

The manuscript studies a narrow logic-language question: how a common noun
whose identity criterion is represented by a setoid can be presented through a
quotient higher inductive type.  Its contribution is a Rocq-checked semantic
fragment with a conditional representation theorem and three linguistic tests:
passenger/person identity, modified common nouns, and book/copredication.  The
paper makes explicit both its formal assumptions and its limits; it does not
claim a native quotient-HIT implementation or a general dot-type coercion
theory.

We believe the manuscript fits JoLLI because it connects formal semantics of
natural-language common nouns with logical and computational foundations of
type theory.

The corresponding author confirms that the work is original, is not under
consideration elsewhere, and has been approved by the author and the relevant
responsible authority at the institution where the work was carried out.
